Food Items

Veg Items

Mattar paneer

Mattar paneer (Hindi: मटर पनीर), also known as matar paneer, muttar paneer, and mutter paneer,[2][3] is a modern restaurant-style and vegetarian punjabi dish[4][5] consisting of peas and paneer in a tomato-based sauce, spiced with garam masala.

Paneer butter masala

Paneer makhani (also called paneer butter masala) is an Indian dish of paneer, originating in New Delhi, in which the gravy is prepared usually with butter (makhan), tomatoes and cashews. Spices such as red chili powder and garam masala are also used to prepare this gravy.

Pav bhaji

Pav bhaji, Paw bhaji or Pao bhaji is a main course staple food of Mumbai, India consisting of a thick spicy vegetable curry served with a soft buttered bread roll. It originated in the city of Mumbai, Maharashtra.

Baingan bharta

Baigan bharta, also spelled baingan bharta or baigan chokha is an Indian dish prepared by mashing or mincing grilled eggplant with tomato, onion, herbs and spices, with variations being common from chef to chef

Non veg Items

Chicken biryani

Biryani is a mixed rice dish, mainly popular in South Asia. It is made with rice, some type of meat and spices. To cater to vegetarians, in some cases, it is prepared by substituting vegetables or paneer for the meat. Sometimes eggs and/or potatoes are also added.

Fish fry

A fish fry is a meal containing battered or breaded fried fish. It usually also includes french fries, coleslaw, macaroni salad, lemon slices, tartar sauce, hot sauce, malt vinegar and dessert. Some Native American versions are cooked by coating fish with semolina and egg yolk.

Chilli chicken

Chilli chicken is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that uses chicken, and is of Hakka Chinese heritage. In India, this may include a variety of dry chicken preparations. Though mainly boneless chicken is used in this dish, some recipes also use bone-in chicken

Chicken curry

Chicken curry or curry/curried chicken is a South Asian dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is common in the cuisine of the Indian subcontinent, Caribbean, Southeast Asia, Great Britain, and Kenya
